Quality Control

Once the protocols related to data sharing have been established, a process to ensure that the data provided by agencies is of standard quality is required. This could be achieved by automating the process of extracting, loading and transforming the data in accordanace with the standard or by developing tools to evaluate the quality of the data provided.

Opportunities

The California Data Collaborative has created a standard protocol for water rate information called the Open Water Rate Specification (OWRS) and is in the process of converting inconsistent rate structures into the format. The creation of a comprehensive protocol for water rates is the first step in its adoption across water districts much like the story of Google Transit Feed Specification.
